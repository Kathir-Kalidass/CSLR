\section{Outline of the Thesis}
This thesis is organized into five chapters, each addressing a specific stage of the study. Chapter 1 introduces the communication context, problem definition, objectives, scope, and motivation for the proposed work. Chapter 2 reviews existing literature and related methods in sign language recognition, followed by a critical analysis of current limitations and research gaps. Chapter 3 presents the proposed methodology, including system architecture, module-level design, algorithmic flow, and supporting mathematical formulation.

Chapter 4 describes implementation details, dataset and input handling, experimental setup, and performance-oriented discussion of results. Finally, Chapter 5 summarizes the major contributions of the thesis, discusses practical limitations, and outlines directions for future enhancement. This progression is intended to provide a coherent transition from problem framing to methodological design, empirical validation, and concluding insights.
